REFRESH art/tech

The art and technology community has historically amplified the worst aspects of both the tech world and the art world resulting in a system which undervalues the work of entire populations, and compounds broader cultural oppressions including those directed at women, people of color, people with disabilities, and LGBTQ+ individuals.

Through their refusal to address systemic sexism and eurocentrism institutions like Ars Electronica reinforce this exclusionary status quo.

We say #KissMyArs

Technology has never been more pervasive or more powerful, and art has a tremendous opportunity to open public dialogue around its benefits and tradeoffs. Contemporary practices manifest works which reflect on our political realities and possible futures.

It’s time to REFRESH our ideas of what art and technology can and should be.

Starting from a position of inclusivity, the REFRESH Biennial will highlight artists around the globe with special attention to those populations that have been historically marginalized. The biennial is about demonstrating an alternative vision of what contemporary art and technology is and will bring together a constellation of venues and organizations in New York City to host the exhibitions.
